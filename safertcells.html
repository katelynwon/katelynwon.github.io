Engineering Safer T-Cells for CAR T-Cell Therapy

Using a New Vector System

Details

  • Date:February 23, 2021

  • Topic:Immunology

  • Affiliation:The Knowledge Society

About the Project

While researching onco-immunotherapies, I stumbled upon CAR T-Cell therapy, a revolutionary treatment process that is given to blood cancer patients alongside chemotherapy. Unfortunately, its side effects can lead to hemotoxicity or neurotoxicity. I propose a new vector system for third generation CD-19 CAR T-Cells to reduce the chances of experiencing these side effects. View my article and video below!

Additional Information

Conventional CAR T-Cells are engineered with lentiviral vectors that have short life spans. These vectors are engineered in the form of plasmids, circular DNA molecules found in bacteria cells that could be easily manipulated using biotechnology. These plasmids integrated with a particular gene of interest are introduced in host mammalian cells to produce proteins. NILV-S/MAR vector systems can allow plasmids to survive longer in human cells and reduce chances of suffering from genotoxicity.
